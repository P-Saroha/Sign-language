üß¨ 4. Linguistic Structure of Sign Language

  • Phonology: Handshape, orientation, location, movement, and facial expressions.
  • Morphology: Inflection and derivation of signs.
  • Syntax: Unique word order like "STORE I GO" (ASL).
  • Semantics: Context-based meanings.
  • Pragmatics: Emotional and tone elements via visual cues.

üìö 6. Fingerspelling

Used to spell proper nouns, technical terms, or words without signs. Each alphabet has a unique hand gesture. ASL and BSL differ in finger alphabets.
